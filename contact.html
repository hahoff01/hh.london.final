Coral Bleaching

What is Coral Bleaching?

Coral bleaching is caused because there is excess CO2 in the air and the ocean then absorbs more CO2 than it should. This then changes the acidity of the ocean, which then changes the temperature. When the water gets warmer the coral expells the algea living on them which then causes them to die.

Why is it Bad?

Coral bleaching is a problem because so many animals rely on the reefs (WWF). Coral reefs are a keystone species, which means that if they were to go extinct then it would have a huge impact on the ecosystem.
